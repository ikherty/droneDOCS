# FPV in Serbia Guide

This page contains notes from the FPV community in Serbia: spot maps, chat links, flying regulations, and more.

## Laws

The laws are quite relaxed:

Up to 900 grams - Category 1, registration is only required if:

- You fly above 100 meters
- Near airports
- More than 500m away from yourself
- Over people
- Near people (30m)
- Near prohibited zones
- At night
- The drone is carrying something

👉 [Link to regulations](https://cad.gov.rs/upload/Bespilotni%20vazduhoplovi/Pravilnik_o_bespilotnim_vazduhoplovima_Sl_glasnik_RS_broj_108-15-nova-eng.pdf)

According to Sergei Orekhov, top places worth driving to for flying:

Far away:

1. Tara National Park - on the border with Bosnia near Montenegro (3.5 hours by car from Belgrade);
2. Fruška Gora National Park - not far from Novi Sad, you can take a train there in half an hour and then taxi (or 1 hour by car from Belgrade);
3. Golubac Fortress - a beautiful freestyle location like from simulators, but flying might not be allowed there) (2 hours);
4. Uvac - a place with deep canyons and river valley (3.5 hours);
5. Stara Planina National Park - a mountain park with waterfalls (3.5 hours);

Relatively close:

1. Avala - small hills with a beautiful view of sunset Belgrade (about 30 min).

   > Send invoices to ukprevozin@gmail.com > \*Note for packages outside the EU (China, America, UK, CH, Norway) that are subject to customs. You can buy only packages for which you can pay the tax online because we are no longer able to pay the tax for you.

Select Germany as the delivery country, make sure that European VAT is included in the prices at checkout (19% I think, to avoid issues with German customs), choose a convenient delivery method (since taxes are paid, you can choose free DHL Express, delivery to the warehouse takes a day or two).

6. Monitor the package via DHL. From the moment it arrives at their warehouse, delivery to Serbia takes about a week (maybe faster, maybe a bit longer; they run the truck 4 times a month, nearest dates are usually on the [website](https://deprevoz.com/datumi-polaska-html), but may differ by a day or two).

7. Wait for an SMS to your Serbian number (better not to message Ivan on Viber, everything will arrive) that you specified, or a message on Viber/WhatsApp saying the package has arrived

8. Go to the Ekstra shopping center parking lot (Omladinskih brigada 100), head to the far end from the entrance and look for a yellow van where two people will be handing out packages, pay them 20 euros or however much the delivery costs, and leave with your package.
